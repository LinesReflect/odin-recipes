Reverse-Seared Steak

Description

If you want to show yourself true self-love, you should really learn and master how to reverse-sear a steak!

When you want to treat yourself and maybe a guest, use the recipe below to guide you to heaven. This recipe is about an hour long and makes 2 servings.

Ingredients

  • 1 thick rib eye steak, 2 in (5 cm) preferably USDA prime
  • 1 teaspoon salt, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon pepper, to taste
  • 3 tablespoons avocado oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and smashed
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 200°F/95°C.
  2. Pat the steak dry with a paper towel, and generously season all sides of the steak with salt and pepper.
  3. Transfer to a wire rack on top of a baking sheet, and bake for about 45 minutes to an hour, until the internal temperature reads about 125°F/50˚C for medium-rare. Adjust the bake time if you like your steak more rare or well-done.
  4. Heat the avocado oil in a pan over high heat until smoking.
  5. Sear the steak for 1 minute on one side, then flip.
  6. Add the butter, garlic, rosemary, and thyme, and swirl around the pan. Transfer the garlic and herbs on top of the steak and baste the steak with the butter using a large spoon.
  7. Baste for about 1 minute, then flip the steak with tongs and baste the other side for about 15 seconds.
  8. Turn the steak on its side and cook to render off any excess fat.
  9. Remove the steak from the pan and let rest for 5-10 minutes.

Conclusion

Mmmmmmmmmm

This is a great treat for your birthday, after earning a promotion, or simply for just being a lovely person. If you're cooking the steak to eat later, please note that the steak can stay refrigerated for 3 to 4 days and kept frozen for about 6 months.
